# Seattle GiveCamp Corporate Sponsorship Fundraising Plan

## Overview
This guide outlines a step-by-step strategy to secure corporate sponsorships for Seattle GiveCamp. The goal is to raise **$20,000** to cover food, facilities, and event expenses. Corporate sponsors will serve as the primary funding source.

---

## 🎯 Step 1: Define Your Sponsorship Strategy

### Fundraising Goals
- **Total Event Goal:** $20,000
- **Corporate Sponsorship Target:** $12,000–$15,000

### Sponsorship Tiers
| Tier | Contribution | Target Number | Total |
|------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Platinum | $5,000 | 1 | $5,000 |
| Gold | $2,500 | 2 | $5,000 |
| Silver | $1,000 | 3 | $3,000 |
| Bronze | $500 | 4 | $2,000 |
| **Total Potential** |  |  | **$15,000** |

### Sponsor Benefits
- Logo placement on website and event materials
- Recognition in social media and press releases
- Recruiting and networking opportunities
- Demonstrated commitment to corporate social responsibility (CSR)

---

## 📄 Step 2: Create Sponsorship Materials

### Required Assets
- One-page sponsorship overview (PDF)
- Sponsorship pitch deck (5–8 slides)
- Event website or landing page
- Donation or sponsorship form
- Email templates
- Event logo and branding kit

### Key Messaging
- Seattle GiveCamp delivers real technology solutions to nonprofits.
- The event is entirely volunteer-driven.
- Sponsors gain brand visibility and community goodwill.
- Participation aligns with ESG and CSR initiatives.

## 💻 Step 5: Build a Prospect List

### Option A: Use Existing Lists
- Past Seattle GiveCamp sponsors
- Sponsors of local hackathons and tech events
- Chamber of Commerce directories
- Tech conference sponsor pages

### Option B: Data Sources for Research or Scraping
- LinkedIn company pages
- Eventbrite and Meetup sponsor listings
- Seattle tech company directories
- Public corporate philanthropy pages

### Recommended Tools
- Python (BeautifulSoup, Scrapy)
- Playwright or Selenium
- Hunter.io
- Apollo.io
- RocketReach
- Clearbit
- Clay
- Airtable

### Ethical Considerations
- Respect robots.txt and website terms of service.
- Use publicly available data only.
- Avoid scraping personal email addresses without consent.
- Verify contact details before outreach.

## 📧 Step 7: Launch an Email Campaign

### Outreach Cadence
| Touchpoint | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| Initial Email | Day 1 |
| Follow-Up #1 | Day 5–7 |
| Follow-Up #2 | Day 14 |
| Final Follow-Up | Day 30 |
| LinkedIn Outreach | Parallel to email |

### Email Best Practices
- Personalize each message.
- Keep emails concise and impactful.
- Highlight community impact.
- Include a clear call to action.
- Attach the sponsorship overview.

---

## 🤝 Step 8: Convert Interest into Sponsorships

### Process
1. Respond promptly to inquiries.
2. Schedule a 15–20 minute discovery call.
3. Present sponsorship tiers and benefits.
4. Customize packages if needed.
5. Send a formal proposal or invoice.
6. Secure commitment and payment.
7. Collect logos and branding materials.

---

## 🙌 Step 9: Stewardship and Retention

### Sponsor Recognition
- Logo placement on website and signage
- Social media mentions
- Acknowledgment during the event
- Inclusion in press releases

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- Send thank-you messages.
- Provide receipts and documentation.
- Share an impact report.
- Include photos and success stories.
- Invite sponsors to participate next year.
